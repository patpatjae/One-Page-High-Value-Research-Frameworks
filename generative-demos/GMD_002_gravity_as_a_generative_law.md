### R1. Counterfactual Universe

If apples flew upward,  
the natural world would become unstable.

Animals would face two evolutionary pressures:
- starve (no access to fruit), or  
- evolve flight to chase fruit into the sky.

But the sky is not an infinite sink.  
Upward-moving objects would accumulate somewhere,  
forming another limit.

This means that *upward flight of apples*  
also requires a stabilizing force,  
just in the opposite direction.

### R2. Closure Argument

Whether upward or downward,  
a stable world requires a **consistent attractor direction**.

- If fruit flies upward, there must be an upward attractor.  
- If fruit falls downward, there must be a downward attractor.

Either way, a **unidirectional pull** must exist.

### R3. Evolutionary Evidence

Humans never evolved the need to fly to obtain fruit.

This implies the attractor is **downward**,  
because upward attractors would have forced  
corresponding evolutionary adaptations.

Since no such adaptations occurred,  
downward pull is the correct attractor direction.

Thus:

**Gravity is the rule because  
the world’s evolutionary history confirms the attractor.**

### R4. Fit-to-Low-Dimension Constraint

Living systems adapt to **low-dimensional stability**,  
not to all possible configurations.

If the world had upward attractors,  
life would have evolved differently.

Our actual world is stable precisely because  
the attractor aligns with the **lowest-dimensional survival path**.

### R5. System Consistency

If apples rise,  
human behavior, animal behavior,  
and ecological cycles must all reorganize.

Since such reorganizations did not occur,  
the minimal consistent world  
is one with **downward attraction**.
